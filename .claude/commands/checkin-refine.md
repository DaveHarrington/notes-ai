You are reviewing and refining a quarterly performance check-in document for an engineering manager.

## Input

The user will provide a link to or paste the check-in draft: $ARGUMENTS

If a Slack link or Obsidian note reference is provided, fetch the content first. If the argument is a person's name, look for their people note in the Obsidian vault (`notes`, folder `People`) and find the most recent check-in section.

## Step 1: Read the draft

Load the full check-in document. Read it carefully before analyzing.

## Step 2: Analyze across dimensions

Evaluate the draft against each of these dimensions, providing specific feedback with quotes from the document:

### Clarity & Structure
- Is the overall narrative clear and easy to follow?
- Are accomplishments organized logically (by theme, priority, or chronology)?
- Does each section have a clear purpose?
- Are transitions between topics smooth?

### Impact & Results
- Are achievements quantified with specific metrics where possible?
- Is business impact clearly articulated?
- Are results tied to team or company goals?
- Does the writing emphasize outcomes over activities?

### Completeness
- Are key projects and initiatives covered?
- Are challenges or learnings addressed appropriately?
- Is forward-looking content (goals, priorities) included?
- Are cross-functional collaborations acknowledged?

### Professional Communication
- Is the tone professional yet authentic?
- Is technical jargon used appropriately for the audience?
- Are sentences concise and active (avoiding passive voice)?
- Are there any grammatical or spelling errors?

### Storytelling & Engagement
- Does the check-in tell a compelling story of the quarter?
- Are there specific examples that bring accomplishments to life?
- Is the writing engaging rather than a dry list?
- Does it balance confidence with appropriate humility?

## Step 3: Section-by-section feedback

For each section of the document, provide:

1. **Strengths** — What's working well?
2. **Improvement opportunities** — What could be stronger?
3. **Specific suggestions** — Concrete rewrites or additions

## Step 4: Red flags check

Scan for and flag any of these issues:
- [ ] Vague statements without supporting details
- [ ] Overuse of buzzwords or corporate jargon
- [ ] Missing metrics or measurable outcomes
- [ ] Overly modest or underselling achievements
- [ ] Inconsistent formatting or structure
- [ ] Lack of ownership (too much "we" without "I")
- [ ] No acknowledgment of setbacks or learning moments

For each flag found, quote the specific text and explain the issue.

## Step 5: Sample rewrites

Provide 2-3 examples of specific sentences or paragraphs from the draft rewritten to be more impactful, clear, or concise. Format as:

**Original:**
> [quoted text from draft]

**Suggested rewrite:**
> [improved version]

**Why:** [brief explanation of what changed and why it's stronger]

## Step 6: Summary

Close with:
- **Overall assessment**: 1-2 sentences on the draft's quality
- **Top 3 priorities**: The three highest-impact changes to make
- **Ready to share?**: Yes / Needs minor edits / Needs significant revision

Print all feedback to the console. Do not modify the original document — the user will decide what changes to incorporate.
